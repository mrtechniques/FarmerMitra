# Large Farm Survey Notes

## What This Feature Does

The Large Farm Survey feature helps farmers analyze an entire farm section instead of checking just one leaf at a time.

It allows the user to:
- Upload multiple leaf photos from different parts of the farm
- Read GPS coordinates from the photos
- Detect disease or healthy plant status for each location
- Place the results on a farm map
- View which zones are healthy, diseased, or at risk
- Compare current survey results with previous scans

In simple words: this feature turns many individual leaf scans into one field-level health map.

## Why This Is Novel

Most plant disease apps stop at single-image diagnosis. They tell you what is wrong with one leaf, but they do not help you understand what is happening across the whole farm.

Our novelty is that we move from:
- leaf diagnosis
to
- spatial farm intelligence

What makes it different:
- It connects AI disease detection with map-based visualization
- It uses photo GPS data to locate problems on the field
- It identifies nearby healthy zones that may become risky because they are close to infected areas
- It supports batch surveying, which is much more useful for medium and large farms
- It helps farmers make location-based decisions, not just image-based decisions

This is important because farming decisions happen at field scale, not at single-leaf scale.

## Why It Is Strong in a Demo

- multiple farm points

Farmers do not inspect only one plant. They need to know where the problem is and how far it has spread.

- time
- pesticide cost
- labor

### 3. Preventive value
The feature does not only show infected areas. It also highlights nearby healthy areas that may be at risk.

### 4. Scalable design
This works better for larger farms than traditional one-image disease apps.

### 5. Strong product thinking
It combines:
- AI detection
- geolocation
- batch workflow
- visual mapping
- historical comparison

That combination makes the product feel complete and practical.
